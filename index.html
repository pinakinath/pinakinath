Welcome (স্বাগতম)

Before my current position, I was an NBHM Postdoctoral Fellow in Mathematics at the Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru, India.

My research interests lie broadly in algebraic geometry. More specifically, I work on algebraic groups, flag varieties, Schubert varieties, Bott–Samelson varieties, toric varieties, spherical varieties, combinatorics of Weyl groups, and aspects of representation theory from a geometric point of view.

For more information, please see my CV, MathSciNet, Google Scholar, and arXiv.

Research

An algebraic group is an algebraic variety endowed with a group structure such that the natural multiplication and inversion maps are morphisms. The theory of algebraic groups plays a central role in several branches of mathematics, including Schubert geometry, geometric representation theory, and the theory of principal bundles over projective varieties.

Schubert and flag varieties are fundamental objects in algebraic geometry, representation theory, and algebraic combinatorics. They are connected with the Borel–Bott–Weil theorem via the cohomology of line bundles. In general, Schubert varieties are singular, and their resolutions—such as Bott–Samelson resolutions—play a significant role in the study of their geometry.

Schubert cycles form a basis of the (co)homology of Grassmannians and flag varieties. The study of their intersection theory dates back to Hermann Schubert and Zeuthen in the 19th century, under the heading of enumerative geometry.

For further details, see: Schubert calculus, Enumerative geometry, Hermann Schubert.
